# 👤 Research — User Personas

Create detailed user personas based on project context, research, and team knowledge. Personas help teams empathize with users and make better design decisions.

## Persona Structure

For each persona, define:
- **Name & Photo**: Make them real and memorable
- **Role/Title**: What do they do?
- **Goals**: What are they trying to accomplish?
- **Frustrations**: What problems do they face?
- **Behaviors**: How do they work/use products?
- **Pain Points**: Where do they struggle?
- **Tech Comfort**: How tech-savvy are they?
- **Typical Day**: Paint a picture of their context
- **Use Cases**: How would they use this product?

Example persona:

```markdown
# Persona 1: Designer Diana

**Role**: Product Designer at fast-paced startup
**Tech Comfort**: Advanced (uses Figma, Framer, Terminal)

## Goals
- Create prototypes quickly for user testing
- Maintain design system consistency across team
- Reduce revision cycles in development

## Frustrations
- Design hand-off to developers is slow
- Design inconsistencies creep in over time
- Hard to manage multiple brand variants

## Behaviors
- Works in tight sprints (1-2 week cycles)
- Collaborates heavily with product and engineering
- Values efficiency over perfection

## Pain Points
- Recreating design components in code
- Keeping design specs in sync with implementation
- Testing multiple brands simultaneously

## Typical Day
8am - Stand-up, review feedback on yesterday's designs
9am - Iterate design based on user testing results
11am - Hand off spec to frontend developer
1pm - Meet with PO to define next week's features
3pm - Update design system with new patterns
5pm - Review dev progress on yesterday's components
```

Output: 3-5 detailed personas representing your target users.
